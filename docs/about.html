About Tender Reach

Procurement shouldn't require an insider to understand it.

Most tender notices are written for people who already know the system. Tender Reach exists for everyone else — the supplier who's never had someone walk them through it before.

Why we exist

Zimbabwe's Procurement Regulatory Authority (PRAZ) runs a real, functioning electronic procurement system — tenders, awards, and procurement plans are all published there. But publishing information isn't the same as making it usable. Dense regulatory language, no plain-language guidance, and no alerts mean smaller and rural suppliers often miss opportunities entirely, or lose out simply because they didn't understand what was being asked of them.

Tender Reach doesn't replace or compete with PRAZ's system. We built a companion layer on top of what's already public — one that speaks the way a helpful, knowledgeable friend would, not the way a regulation does.

How Tender Reach actually works

Everything happens over WhatsApp, because that's where suppliers already are — no new app to download, no login to remember.

1. Ask a procurement question

Not sure how to register as a supplier, or what a specific clause means? Ask in plain language. We're building toward instant AI-assisted answers; today, a real person on our team responds within 24 hours.

2. Get a tender explained

Forward a tender notice you've found — from PRAZ's portal, the Government Gazette, or anywhere else — and we'll return a plain-language summary: what's required, the deadline, and a document checklist, without the legal phrasing getting in the way.

3. Learn what we're building

Tender discovery, document guidance, supplier support, and AI-assisted answers — all aimed at one goal: making procurement clear, fair, and accessible for every business in Zimbabwe, not just the ones who already know the ropes.

The Founding Supplier Programme

Before building more, we want to understand real procurement experience from real suppliers — not guess at it. That's what the Founding Supplier Programme is for.

What joining involves

A short, conversational interview on WhatsApp — about 8–10 minutes — covering how you currently find tenders, what's hardest about applying, and what one thing would help you most. It's a conversation, not a form: you can stop at any point, and whatever you've shared up to then still helps shape what we build.

What you get

  • Free, priority access to plain-language tender summaries and deadline reminders — no paywall, ever, for this core support.
  • Direct input into what Tender Reach builds next. Your answers shape our actual product roadmap, not just a feedback form nobody reads.
  • Recognition as a founding member once we launch publicly, only if you choose to be named.

What we ask in return

Just honesty. We're not looking for suppliers who've had a perfect experience — we're looking to understand where the system is genuinely hard to navigate, so we can actually fix it.

What we won't do

  • We don't scrape or bulk-republish PRAZ's procurement data — we work only with content you share with us directly, or information that's already meant to be public.
  • We don't sell your information, publish a supplier "score" or rating, or share your data with anyone outside the team.
  • We don't charge for the core supplier-support features. If a paid tier is ever introduced, it won't gate the basic help every supplier deserves.

Our commitments

Four principles guide every decision we make about data, access, and design:

  • Transparent — we tell you plainly what we collect and why, before we collect it.
  • Accountable — every claim we publish has a way for you to respond or correct it.
  • Secure — your data is never sold, and you can ask us to delete it at any time.
  • Reliable — built for suppliers with real connectivity constraints, not just the best-connected ones.
